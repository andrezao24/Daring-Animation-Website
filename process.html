I. Pre-Production

Brainstorming

In our brainstorming, we came up with three different stories. We debated the ideas and, after analyzing some details like the characters' backgrounds, we decided which story we wanted to make. Our focus was on creating an empathetic main character named Jesse, who is dealing with grief and addiction.

Research

During our research, we studied the psychology of grief and addiction to make the project authentic. We also researched how colors in lighting transmit certain emotions, just like we read in our documents. For example, we learned that blue can show distance and passivity, while red can highlight anxiety or conflict.

Character Development

For character development, we used the "Creating Empathic Characters" method to build the hero's journey. We created detailed backgrounds for everyone, defining things like their birth dates, where they are from, their height, and all their physical and psychological traits. For example, we defined that Jesse is 25 years old and struggles with a deep fear of starting relationships.

Scripting

Scripting was the process of writing the actual screenplay. It went through several changes, and we kept writing and updating it consistently while the storyboard was also being made. Every line was written to match the emotional tone of the story.

Script Test

The script test was simply the process of reviewing the script to catch any errors or inconsistencies. We read through everything to see if our ideas made sense and to refine the story before moving to the next phase.

Storyboard

We developed the storyboard in a simple but conclusive way using hand-drawn sketches. We planned out all the camera angles, the character movements, and the initial ideas for the environmental colors to prepare for the visual production.

Moodboard

We created a moodboard to define our overall visual style. For our visual identity, we were heavily inspired by the 3D hand-painted and digital painting style of the show Arcane by Fortiche Productions. We also used AI to help us organize this moodboard so it matched our exact vision.

Concept Art

For the concept art, we designed the environments, characters, and assets based on our chosen visual style. Everything, from Jesse's messy room to the guitar with the worn-out sticker his grandfather gave him, was designed to keep visual consistency with our hand-painted look.

II. Production

Modeling

For modeling, we built all our 3D assets in Blender. To create the base character geometry and their clothes, we used add-ons like MPFB, which really helped speed up our workflow. We also manually modeled all the specific environmental clutter. For example, to really show Jesse's state of mind, we carefully modeled his messy room, adding scattered wine bottles, tobacco boxes, trash, and torn posters to the environment. We also modeled important props, like the acoustic guitar his grandfather gave him for his 14th birthday, complete with the worn-out sticker.

Texturing

For texturing, we applied our signature style using procedural node setups and hand-painted techniques. We focused heavily on our 3D hybrid style of manual and digital painting to give the project a distinct artistic identity.

Rigging

In the rigging phase, we built the internal skeletons for our characters. To make this process more efficient, we used Mixamo and the MPFB add-on to help generate the base setups. From there, we established complex rigs to support performance-heavy sequences, making sure the skeletons could handle all the subtle, heavy body language required for a story dealing with deep grief and addiction.

Casting & Contracts

For casting, we sourced voice talent and managed all the legal agreements, including NDAs to protect our script and project details. We integrated high-quality vocal performances, making sure the actors matched our detailed character voice guides. For instance, we worked with Donovan Reynolds to capture Jesse's rough, tired voice, and Mike Harrison-Wood to deliver the warm, stable, and grounding tone of his grandfather, Joseph.

Music

For the soundtrack, we also managed the legal agreements to license the music for the short film. We worked out a contract with Ricardo Pinto to secure the rights to the tracks. To fit the intimate and emotional tone of Jesse's story, the score consists entirely of acoustic guitar parts, featuring several songs from the band Blonde Fragility.

Animation

During animation, we combined Mixamo with manual keyframe data to ensure the characters' gestures and expressions truly supported their internal emotional states. For example, we had to animate Jesse to reflect his incredibly low energy, making sure his movements physically showed the exhaustion and effort it takes for him just to speak or move.

Lighting

We crafted the atmosphere through lighting, rendering everything in Eevee to maintain real-time visual fidelity. We relied heavily on the psychological color theory we researched, using specific colored lights to trigger emotions, like using blue lighting to highlight Jesse's introspection and emotional distance, or red to emphasize moments of anxiety and conflict.

III. Post-Production

Editing & Color Grading

This was our assembly phase. In DaVinci Resolve, we cut the narrative together and applied the final color grading. We carefully adjusted the hues and saturation to act as visual adjectives, ensuring the colors enhanced the emotional weight of the story and reflected the different stages of the characters' journeys.

Social Media

Finally, we created social media accounts to promote our short film. We identified Instagram and TikTok as our main platforms because we know our target audience, young adults between 18 and 25 years old, is highly active there, which gives us the best chance for engagement. YouTube is also being used as a secondary platform to support our video content
